The Pattern

The Research Gap

"I tried researching examples of a founding team taking a premium water source from initial discovery to major financial event — and could not find a single example in the last 100 years."

"Much more common is what you see with Saratoga. The playbook is basically:"

1
The founding team cultivates a source and builds a brand story that resonates locally.
2
Brand gains traction — demand exceeds local distribution capacity.
3
They hit a wall with scale. Distribution economics squeeze the producer. The brand enters distress.
4
A distribution consolidator acquires the asset — which is in distress. Founding team is out.

"This pattern exists for the most high-profile brands which eventually achieve scale."

The Provocation

"Does it ever make sense to have a water on the menu offered for over $100?"

Vintage water. Special edition. Limited source. The answer is almost certainly yes — and AI is what makes the case for it compelling.

Final Thoughts

AI is a godsend
to this industry.

It has the potential to disrupt the economic reality that has been in place for over 100 years.
But to take advantage, we need new ways of thinking:

PLATFORM Think in terms of a premium water platform or network — not the isolated scaling of an individual brand
PRICING Think in terms of perceived value to achieve premium pricing — value-based pricing leveraging AI
TECHNOLOGY The role of technology is disintermediation — displacing the traditional forces between producer and consumer
