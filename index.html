The Science of Sensation

Water that you feel,
not just drink

Mineral composition isn't chemistry — it's character. The minerals in your water shape taste, mouthfeel, and even emotion.

"The difference between still water and perfectly mineralized water is the difference between background music and a symphony — once you've heard it, you can't unhear it."

Ca²⁺

Calcium

The backbone of body and mouthfeel. Higher calcium creates a sensation of substance and depth — the feeling of water that satisfies on a cellular level.

Mg²⁺

Magnesium

A gentle brightness that lifts the palate. Magnesium is what makes water feel alive — it adds a mineral liveliness that pairs beautifully with white wine and delicate cuisine.

HCO₃⁻

Bicarbonate

The great neutralizer. Bicarbonate softens acidity, smooths the palate, and creates water with a silky, rounded character — ideal for food-forward events.

Si

Silica

The essence of softness. Silica gives water its most luxurious quality — a slip, a smoothness, a sense that you're drinking something genuinely fine.

Na⁺

Sodium

Depth and salinity. A precise touch of sodium creates water with dimension — evoking ocean air, coastal settings, and a taste of the wild.

pH

pH & Balance

The conductor of the ensemble. pH determines how all other minerals express themselves — our profiles are calibrated to a precise balance that serves the moment.

Total Dissolved Solids —
the signature of a water

TDS — the measure of all dissolved minerals in water — is a water sommelier's primary vocabulary. A low TDS creates something ethereal and pure. A high TDS creates something bold and complex.

We calibrate each water's TDS not by a generic preference, but by what your specific experience calls for — the terroir of your moment.

TDS Spectrum — Character Profiles
Ethereal 0–50 mg/L
Delicate 50–250 mg/L
Classic 250–800 mg/L
Rich 800–1500 mg/L
Bold 1500+ mg/L
